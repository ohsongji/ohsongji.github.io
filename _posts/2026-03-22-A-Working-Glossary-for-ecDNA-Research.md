This post is a personal glossary of core molecular biology concepts frequently used in genome engineering and extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA) research.

---

## 🧬 DNA Structure and Basic Molecular Biology

- **DNA**: Deoxyribonucleic acid is the molecule that carries genetic information in cells.
- **Nucleotide**: The basic building block of DNA composed of a base, sugar, and phosphate group.
- **Base pair**: Two complementary nucleotides in DNA that pair through hydrogen bonding.
- **Double helix**: The twisted structure formed by two complementary DNA strands.
- **Antiparallel**: DNA strands run in opposite directions (5' → 3', 3' → 5').
- **5' end**: The end of a DNA strand that has a free phosphate group.
- **3' end**: The end of a DNA strand that has a free hydroxyl group.
![DNA structure](/assets/dna-structure.png)

*Figure 1. DNA structure (source: Labster, https://theory.labster.com/dna-structure/)*

- **phosphodiester bond**: The chemical bond linking nucleotides in a DNA strand.
- **Template strand**: The DNA strand used as a template for DNA or RNA synthesis.
- **Complementary strand**: A DNA strand with bases that pair with another strand according to base pairing rules.
- **Genome**: The complete set of genetic material in an organism.
- **Chromosome**: A DNA molecule containing genetic information packaged with proteins.
- **Chromatin**: The complex of DNA wrapped around histones.
- **Histones**: Proteins that help package DNA into nucleosomes.
- **Gene**: A DNA sequence that encodes a functional product such as a protein or RNA.
- **Promoter**: A DNA sequence that initiates transcription of a gene.
- **Enhancer**: A regulatory DNA element that increases gene transcription.
- **Transcription factor**: A protein that regulates gene expression by binding DNA.
- **mRNA**: Messenger RNA that carries genetic information from DNA to ribosomes.
- **Transcription**: The process of synthesizing RNA from a DNA template.
- **Translation**: The process of synthesizing proteins from mRNA.

## 🔁 DNA Replication

- **DNA replication**: The process by which a cell copies its DNA before cell division.
- **Origin of replication**: A DNA sequence where replication begins.
- **Replication fork**: Y-shaped structure where DNA strands separate.
- **Helicase**: Enzyme that unwinds DNA.
- **DNA polymerase**: Enzyme that synthesizes DNA.
- **Leading strand**: The DNA strand synthesized continuously during replication.
- **Lagging strand**: The DNA strand synthesized discontinuously.
- **Okazaki fragments**: Short DNA fragments synthesized on the lagging strand.

---

## 🧨 DNA Damage and Repair

- **Double-strand break (DSB)**: Break in both DNA strands.
- **Single-strand break (SSB)**: Break in one strand.
- **Homologous recombination (HR)**: Repair using homologous DNA.
- **Non-homologous end joining (NHEJ)**: Direct ligation without template.
- **Microhomology-mediated end joining (MMEJ)**: A repair pathway that uses short homologous sequences during repair.
- **Base excision repair (BER)**: A DNA repair pathway that removes and replaces damaged or chemically modified bases to maintain genome integrity (repairs small base lesions).
- **Nucleotide excision repair (NER)**: Repairs bulky DNA damage.
- **Ligation**: Enzymatic process of joining two DNA fragments by forming a phosphodiester bond between their ends.

## ✂️ Nucleases and DNA cutting
- **Nuclease**: An enzyme that cleaves phosphodiester bonds in DNA or RNA.
- **Endonuclease**: A nuclease that cuts DNA within a strand.
- **Exonuclease**: A nuclease that removes nucleotides from DNA ends.
- **Restriction enzyme**: An endonuclease that cuts DNA at specific sequences.
- **Nickase**: An enzyme that cuts only one strand of DNA.

## ✂️ Genome Editing

- **CRISPR**: Genome editing technology for targeted DNA modification.
- **Cas9**: RNA-guided nuclease.
- **sgRNA**: RNA guiding Cas9 to target DNA.
- **PAM sequence**: A short DNA motif required for Cas nucleases to bind DNA.
- **Knockout**: Gene disruption.
- **Knock-in**: Gene insertion.
- **Donor template**: A DNA construct used for homology-directed repair.
- **Homology arm**: DNA sequences that match the genomic target site during HDR.
---

## 🧬 ecDNA Biology

- **Extrachromosomal DNA**: Circular DNA outside chromosomes containing amplified oncogenes.
- **eccDNA**: Extrachromosomal circular DNA derived from chromosomal sequences.
- **Double minutes**: Small circular ecDNA fragments in cancer cells.
- **Chromothripsis**: Massive chromosome fragmentation event.
- **Oncogene amplification**: An increase in oncogene copy number that enhances tumor growth.
- **Break-fusion-bridge cycle**: A mechanism generating genomic amplification and rearrangement.
